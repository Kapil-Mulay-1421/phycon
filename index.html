About the Conference

PHYCON-26, the National Physics Conference, will be hosted at IIT Ropar, Punjab, India, bringing together researchers, academicians, and students from across the country to showcase and discuss recent advances in both theoretical and experimental physics. Building on the resounding success of PHYCON-25, the conference continues its mission of fostering high-quality scientific exchange, collaboration, and innovation.

Organized annually, PHYCON provides a vibrant platform for researchers, academicians, and students to exchange ideas, present their latest findings, and explore emerging frontiers in physics. The conference features recent advances in light–matter interactions, quantum information technology, condensed matter physics, materials science, nuclear and particle physics, and gravity and string theory.

PHYCON-26 aims to inspire young researchers, particularly PhD students, to pursue high-quality and creative research in India. It also offers opportunities to interact with leading physicists and industry professionals through plenary talks, workshops while providing a respected platform for research presentations through oral and poster sessions. The conference will also include specialized workshops and industry-focused talks, aimed at enhancing practical exposure and strengthening academia–industry interaction. By fostering collaboration and knowledge sharing, PHYCON-26 seeks to contribute to future scientific progress in the country.

About the Department of Physics

Established in 2009, the Department of Physics, IIT Ropar has steadily evolved into a vibrant centre for teaching, research, and innovation. Guided by a strong commitment to academic excellence, the department strives to nurture curiosity-driven learning while pursuing cutting-edge research that addresses both fundamental questions and emerging technological challenges.

Our research spans four major thrust areas: Light–Matter Interactions and Quantum Information Technology, Condensed Matter Physics and Materials Science, Nuclear and Particle Physics, and Gravity and String Theory. Through these fields, we aim to advance the frontiers of scientific knowledge and contribute to discoveries that deepen our understanding of the physical world and its future directions.
